Design Thinking Final Project: BC's Printing System

This project was presented as the final for a Design Thinking elective in CSOM, taught by Kristie Dickenson. As the course progressed through the human-centered design process, we would complete the parallel section of our project. I chose to tackle printing at BC because procrastination with this process often contributes to students being late for classes. Also, while some majors print much more than others, the majority of BC students need to print out papers, articles, homework, etc.

After considering the project's budgetary constraints, I realized that I would need to address student awarness instead of attempting to hold a full scale assult on procrastination, scheduling, and choice of workspace. The three prototypes below are all meant to build awareness, whether for wait-times at specific printers or where printers are located on campus.

Final Version

For my final version of the project, I used elements from a few of my prototypes. The map shows all of the printers on main campus (along with 2000 Comm Ave.) and is acompanied by a key and a QR code that directs a user to the printing homepage on BC's website. The use of a large, bold san-sarif with heavy contrast was intented to provide a clean read.
